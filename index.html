Vision and Image Processing Lab

Vision and Image Processing Lab is a research facility in the Electrical Engineering Department dedicated to Deep Learning, haptics, computer vision and image processing applications. Research activities include: Co-segmentation, Re-identification, Learning strategies in Deep learning, Anomaly Detection, Data compression, Few shot learning, Distance metric learning, Haptic rendering, Haptic communication and Haptic perception.

Vision and Deep Learning

Research in Vision based Deep Learning takes place in this lab in a very broad spectrum. The major areas of research include: Co-segmentation, Person reidentification, Anomaly detection, Data compression, Few shot and zero shot learning, Distance Metric learning, Incremental Learning etc

Read More

Haptics

Haptics research in this lab ranges from Haptic rendering: getting tactile and force feedback of the virtual scenes, Haptic communication: designing protocols for transmitting haptic data packets, to Haptic perception where machine learning algorithms are used to distinguish between different haptic feedbacks.

About us

"Research is to see what everybody else has seen, and to think what nobody else has thought." - Albert Szent-Gyorgyi

Vision and Image Processing Lab is a research facility in the Electrical Department of IIT Bombay, dedicated to cutting edge research in Deep Learning, haptics, computer vision and image processing applications. Major research in the lab is focussed on the following topics:

1. Co-segmentation

2. Person Re-Identification

3. Data Sensing and Compression

4. Anomaly detection

5. Few Shot Learning

6. Incremental Learning

7. Distance Metric learning

8. Haptic Rendering

9. Haptic Communication

10. Haptic Perception

Computing Power

Several high-performance GPU stacks accommodate the demand for immense computing power in the lab.
